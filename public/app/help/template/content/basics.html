Profile Basics

Do I need to create a profile?

When you visit the site Home Page for the first time, your account will be created automatically. When you click on “My Profile” in the Home Page, your Profile Page will be created automatically based on existing information stored in other HSBC databases. You will not have to go through a separate process for creating a Profile Page.

What will I see on my profile?

  • Your name, department name, role, region of employment, and Lotus Notes ID are captured from Active Directory.
  • If you have uploaded a photograph to the Staff Photograph Repository via HRDirect, then the photograph will also be displayed.
  • If you have not yet added professional skills onto your SMART Profile via GMIS2G, then the “Skills” section of your Profile Page will be blank. If you have added skills onto your SMART Profile via GMIS2G, then the skills will be displayed in the “Skills” section of your Profile Page.
  • When you visit your Profile Page for the first time, the “Interests” section will display the word “NONE”.
  • By default, you will be marked as actively seeking for opportunities.
  • If there are opportunities available that require skills matching the ones displayed in the “Skills” section, then these opportunities will be listed in the “You might be interested in the following projects” section.

My skills are colour coded and they appear inside bubbles. How does this work?

The larger the bubble in the “Skills” tab, the stronger the expertise.
The skills are grouped under the following categories:
  • “Business Domain” skills will appear in red bubbles
  • “HSBC Application” skills will appear in orange bubbles
  • “Management” skills will appear in yellow bubbles
  • “Project Management” skills will appear in green bubbles
  • “Soft Skills” will appear in blue bubbles
  • “Technical” skills will appear in purple bubbles
The scale used for the skills are adapted from GMIS2G SMART:
  • 1-Novice: New to the job. Is able to do the job but unable to meet the standards and requires total supervision.
  • 2-Learner: A person who is a beginner and is able to do the job, though not yet consistently, to the required standards and needs frequent supervision. This indicates the person needs much help and training to do the job.
  • 3-Proficient: A person who has some experience and is able to do the job to the required standards consistently, on a routine basis with occasional supervision. This indicates the person can do without much help to do the job.
  • 4-Professional: An experienced person who is able to do the job to the required standards consistently without any supervision.
  • 5-Expert: A person who is recognized as an authority for his/her ability to do the job to exemplary standards independently and one who is also able to coach others.
